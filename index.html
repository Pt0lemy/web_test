The Problem

So What is the problem with plastic pollution?

The problem with plastic pollution is that plastic doesn't break down easily, so it builds up in our environment and causes harm to wildlife, ecosystems, and even human health. Every year, millions of tons of plastic waste end up in our oceans, rivers, and landfills. Animals often mistake plastic for food or get tangled in it, which can injure or kill them. Microplastics—tiny pieces of plastic—can also enter the food chain and potentially harm people. Since plastic is made from fossil fuels, producing it also contributes to climate change. Reducing plastic use, recycling properly, and finding alternatives can help fight this growing problem.

Plastic production has grown rapidly because it's cheap and convenient, but most of it is used only once and then thrown away. Single-use plastics like bags, bottles, straws, and packaging make up a huge portion of the waste found in nature. Since many places lack proper waste management systems, plastic often ends up littering streets, filling landfills, or flowing into rivers and oceans.

Actions to reduce pollution

Use less single-use plastic, Recycle properly, Support plastic bans and laws, Pick up litter, Buy products with less plastic.
